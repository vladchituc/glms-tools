Magnitude Estimation — Fixed Modulus
For this kind of rating, we'll ask you to describe the badness of a crime by directly comparing it to a different crime: stealing a wallet.

We want you to first just give that crime any number that feels right to you. What does matter is that you assign the other crimes a number in relationship to stealing a wallet. Suppose you gave it a "12."

If another crime is just as bad as stealing a wallet, then you should also give it a 12. If it is half as bad, you should give it a 6. If it's ten times as bad, give it a 120, and so on.

Minimal variant
